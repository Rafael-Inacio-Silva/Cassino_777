A) How to play - top

Overview

French Roulette is a classic casino game that offers players an exciting and strategic experience. The game is similar to American Roulette but with key differences that can influence your chances of winning.

Objective

The objective of French Roulette is to predict where the ball will land on the spinning roulette wheel. You can place various types of bets, each with different odds and payouts.

How to Play

  1. Place Your Bets: Players place their bets on the roulette table. The betting options include individual numbers, groups of numbers, colors (red or black), odd or even numbers, and high (19-36) or low (1-18) numbers.
  2. Spin the Wheel: Once all bets are placed, the croupier spins the roulette wheel in one direction and rolls a small ball in the opposite direction.
  3. Outcome: The ball eventually lands in one of the numbered pockets on the wheel. The winning number and color are then announced, and payouts are made based on the bets placed.

Key Differences from American Roulette

Single Zero: French Roulette features a wheel with 37 pockets, including numbers 1 through 36 and a single zero (0). In contrast, American Roulette has 38 pockets with an additional double zero (00), which slightly reduces the player's odds.

Special Rule: La Partage

French Roulette incorporates the La Partage rule, which benefits players when the ball lands on zero (0). If you have placed an even-money bet (e.g., red/black, odd/even, high/low) and the ball lands on zero, you will receive half of your stake back. This rule reduces the house edge and increases your chances of retaining some of your bet even in unfavorable outcomes.
